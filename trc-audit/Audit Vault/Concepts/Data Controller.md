# Data Controller

## What it is (plain English)
A data controller is the organisation that decides why and how personal data is processed. The controller carries the bulk of GDPR accountability: choosing a [[Lawful Basis]], drafting the privacy notice, signing off [[DPIA]]s, appointing processors, and responding to [[Data Subject Rights]] requests.

## What it means for TRC
For candidate data in Firefish and client/contact data in Odoo, TRC is the controller — Firefish, Odoo, Anthropic, Microsoft, and Railway act as [[Data Processor]]s under instruction. The audit will verify that this controller status is reflected in every [[Data Processing Agreement]], in the privacy notice on the TRC website, and in the [[GDPR Article 30 - ROPA]] entries owned by the [[DPO]].

## When it applies
Already in force. Controller status is a matter of factual reality (who decides the purposes and means), so it cannot be contracted away even if a vendor's standard terms attempt to flip the roles.
