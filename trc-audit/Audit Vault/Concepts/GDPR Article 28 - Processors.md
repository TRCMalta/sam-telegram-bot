# GDPR Article 28 - Processors

## What it is (plain English)
Article 28 of the GDPR governs the relationship between a [[Data Controller]] and a [[Data Processor]]. Whenever a controller hands personal data to a processor, the two must have a written contract — a [[Data Processing Agreement]] — that sets out the subject matter, duration, nature and purpose of processing, the type of personal data, and the controller's instructions. Article 28 also lists mandatory clauses (confidentiality, security, sub-processors, audits, return or deletion of data at the end).

## What it means for TRC
Every supplier that touches TRC personal data on TRC's behalf needs an Article 28-compliant DPA on file: Anthropic, Microsoft, Railway, Firefish, Odoo, and any smaller vendor. The audit will sample the supplier register and ask to see the DPA, the sub-processor list, and evidence that TRC reviewed it. Engaging a new sub-processor without authorisation is the most common Article 28 breach.

## When it applies
In force since 25 May 2018. The duty is triggered the moment TRC shares personal data with a third party that processes it on TRC's instructions.
