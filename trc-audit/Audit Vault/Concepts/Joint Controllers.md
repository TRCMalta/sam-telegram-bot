# Joint Controllers

## What it is (plain English)
Joint controllers are two or more organisations that jointly determine the purposes and means of the same processing activity, as defined in Article 26 of the GDPR. They must enter into a transparent arrangement that allocates responsibilities — especially for handling [[Data Subject Rights]] requests and providing required information to individuals — and the essence of that arrangement must be made available to the data subjects.

## What it means for TRC
Joint controllership can arise unexpectedly with co-marketing partners, jointly run events, or shared candidate pipelines with a sister agency. The audit will check whether any commercial partnership involving shared personal data has been classified, whether a joint-controller arrangement has been signed where the classification fits, and whether the [[DPO]] has confirmed it is not mislabelled as a simple [[Data Processor]] relationship.

## When it applies
Already in force. The trigger is factual — co-decision over purposes and means — so a joint-controller arrangement must be in place before joint processing begins, not retrofitted at audit time.
