# GDPR Chapter V - International Transfers
 
## What it is (plain English)
Chapter V of the GDPR (Articles 44 to 49) governs transfers of personal data outside the European Economic Area. A transfer is only lawful if the destination country has an adequacy decision from the European Commission, or appropriate safeguards are in place (most commonly [[Standard Contractual Clauses]] plus a transfer impact assessment), or a narrow derogation applies. Onward transfers by a [[Data Processor]] to a sub-processor in a third country fall under the same rules.

## What it means for TRC
Several TRC suppliers either are based outside the EEA or use sub-processors that are. Anthropic (US) and Microsoft (US) typically rely on the EU-US Data Privacy Framework adequacy decision plus SCCs as a fallback; Railway (US) usually requires SCCs. The audit will check that for each non-EEA recipient TRC has identified the transfer mechanism, completed a transfer impact assessment, and recorded it in the [[GDPR Article 30 - ROPA]].

## When it applies
In force since 25 May 2018. Triggered the moment personal data leaves the EEA, including by remote access from a third country.
