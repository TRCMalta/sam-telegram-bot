# Data Subject Rights
 
## What it is (plain English)
Data subject rights are the personal rights given to individuals under Articles 12 to 22 of the GDPR. They include the right to be informed, the right of access (a so-called DSAR), the rights to rectification, erasure ("right to be forgotten"), restriction of processing, data portability, objection, and not to be subject to solely automated decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects.

## What it means for TRC
TRC must be able to receive, log, and respond to a data subject request within one calendar month — typically a candidate asking what data is held in Firefish, or a client contact asking for deletion from Odoo. The audit will check that there is a documented intake channel, that the [[DPO]] owns the response template, that records of past requests exist, and that any solely automated decision-making (e.g. AI ranking of candidates) is either avoided or paired with a meaningful human review step.

## When it applies
Already in force. The one-month deadline is hard, extendable by two further months only for genuinely complex or high-volume requests, with the data subject informed of any extension.
