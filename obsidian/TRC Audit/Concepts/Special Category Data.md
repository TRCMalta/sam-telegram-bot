# Special Category Data

## What it is (plain English)
Special category data is the heightened-protection class defined in Article 9 of the GDPR: racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs, trade-union membership, genetic data, biometric data used for identification, health data, and data concerning a person's sex life or sexual orientation. Processing it is prohibited unless one of the specific Article 9(2) conditions is met (explicit consent, employment-law obligations, vital interests, etc.).

## What it means for TRC
In a recruitment context this category appears constantly in incidental ways: health information mentioned in a candidate email, religious references in a CV, trade-union history. TRC must train staff not to capture it into Firefish or Odoo unnecessarily, must not pass it to Anthropic or other AI processors for routine drafting, and must have an Article 9(2) condition recorded where retention is genuinely required — typically employment-law obligations for placed candidates.

## When it applies
Already in force. The Article 9 prohibition has applied since 25 May 2018 and the [[DPO]] should run a periodic sample audit of free-text fields in Firefish to catch accidental capture of special-category data.
