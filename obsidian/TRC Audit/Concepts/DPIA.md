# DPIA

## What it is (plain English)
A Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) is the structured document that proves an organisation has thought through the privacy risks of a new or changed processing activity before starting it. It describes the processing, justifies why it is necessary and proportionate, lists the risks to data subjects, and records the mitigations and residual risk. It is a living document — material changes to the processing should trigger an update.

## What it means for TRC
TRC produces a DPIA whenever it adopts a new AI tool, a new recruitment automation, or any feature that profiles candidates. The [[DPO]] owns the template and signs off the result; the business owner of the activity provides the inputs. The audit will sample DPIAs to check they were completed before launch, that residual risk is acceptable, and that mitigations such as access controls or supplier checks are actually in place.

## When it applies
Required under [[GDPR Article 35 - DPIA]], in force since 25 May 2018. Triggered by any new processing likely to be high risk, including most AI deployments touching personal data.
