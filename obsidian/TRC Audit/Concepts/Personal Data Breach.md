# Personal Data Breach

## What it is (plain English)
A personal data breach is, in GDPR terms, "a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure of, or access to" personal data. It covers far more than malicious hacking — a misdirected email, a lost laptop, a misconfigured Railway environment, or an AI prompt leak can all qualify.

## What it means for TRC
Under Article 33, TRC as [[Data Controller]] must notify the supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware of a notifiable breach, and under Article 34 must notify affected individuals where the risk to them is high. The audit will look for a breach-response runbook owned by the [[DPO]], a breach log (even of incidents that did not meet the notification threshold), and evidence that processors such as Anthropic, Microsoft, Railway, Firefish, and Odoo are contractually obliged to alert TRC without undue delay.

## When it applies
Already in force. The 72-hour clock starts at the moment of awareness, not the moment of confirmation, so the response process must be rehearsed and ready before an incident occurs.
