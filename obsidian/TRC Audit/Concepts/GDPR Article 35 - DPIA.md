# GDPR Article 35 - DPIA

## What it is (plain English)
Article 35 of the GDPR requires a Data Protection Impact Assessment ([[DPIA]]) before any processing that is likely to result in a high risk to people's rights and freedoms. Triggers include systematic large-scale profiling, processing [[Special Category Data]] at scale, systematic monitoring of public areas, and the use of new technologies (which captures most generative AI deployments). The DPIA must describe the processing, assess necessity and proportionality, evaluate risks, and document mitigations.

## What it means for TRC
TRC must run a DPIA before introducing any new AI tool that processes candidate or employee data, before any large-scale change to recruitment automation, and before adopting a new high-risk vendor. The audit will look for a DPIA register, completed DPIAs signed off by the [[DPO]], and evidence that mitigations have actually been implemented. Where a DPIA shows residual high risk, prior consultation with the supervisory authority is required.

## When it applies
In force since 25 May 2018. The duty is triggered before any new high-risk processing begins, not after.
