# Article 27 - FRIA

## What it is (plain English)
Article 27 introduces the Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment (FRIA). Before deploying certain high-risk AI systems, specific categories of deployer must analyse how the system could affect people's fundamental rights, identify the people likely to be affected, the risks to them, the human oversight in place, and the mitigations. The result is filed with the market surveillance authority.

## What it means for TRC
The Article 27 obligation is targeted mainly at public bodies, bodies governed by public law, and private operators delivering essential public services (banking, insurance, etc.). TRC is a private SME outside those categories, so a FRIA is typically not required. The audit position is to document this scoping decision and revisit it if TRC ever takes on public-sector work or deploys an [[Annex III - High-Risk AI Systems]] tool in a covered context. A standard [[DPIA]] under GDPR is still expected where personal data is involved.

## When it applies
Applies from 2 August 2026, but only to deployers in the categories listed in Article 27(1). TRC's current scoping is "not applicable, documented."
