# Data Processor

## What it is (plain English)
A data processor is an organisation that handles personal data strictly on a [[Data Controller]]'s behalf and under that controller's documented instructions. Processors must keep their own [[GDPR Article 30 - ROPA]], must not engage sub-processors without authorisation, must support the controller's response to [[Data Subject Rights]] requests, and must apply appropriate technical and organisational security measures.

## What it means for TRC
TRC's main processors are Anthropic (model inference), Microsoft (productivity and identity), Railway (application hosting), Firefish (ATS), and Odoo (CRM/ERP). Each must be bound by a [[Data Processing Agreement]] under [[GDPR Article 28 - Processors]], with cross-border transfers handled per [[GDPR Chapter V - International Transfers]]. The audit will check the live processor register and confirm the [[DPO]] reviewed the latest sub-processor list for each.

## When it applies
Already in force. The processor's status and duties activate the moment it starts handling TRC personal data, regardless of whether a written DPA has been completed — though the absence of a DPA is itself a breach of Article 28.
