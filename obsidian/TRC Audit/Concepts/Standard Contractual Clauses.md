# Standard Contractual Clauses

## What it is (plain English)
Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) are the pre-approved template contract terms published by the European Commission that organisations use to lawfully transfer personal data to countries outside the EEA. The current version is the 2021 modular SCCs, which have to be combined with a Transfer Impact Assessment that checks whether the destination country's surveillance and access regime would undermine the protection on paper.

## What it means for TRC
Wherever TRC sends personal data to a non-EEA vendor that is not covered by an adequacy decision or a recognised framework (e.g. the EU-US Data Privacy Framework for certified Anthropic and Microsoft entities), an SCC-based [[Data Processing Agreement]] plus a Transfer Impact Assessment is the default safeguard. The audit will check that the right modules are picked (Module 2 controller-to-processor for most TRC cases), that annexes list the actual data categories, and that the [[DPO]] has signed off the TIA.

## When it applies
Already in force. The 2021 SCCs have been mandatory since 27 December 2022 for new transfers, and TRC must use them whenever a transfer falls under [[GDPR Chapter V - International Transfers]] without an adequacy decision.
