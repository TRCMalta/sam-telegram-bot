# DPO

## What it is (plain English)
The Data Protection Officer (DPO) is the person responsible for advising the organisation on its data protection obligations, monitoring compliance, and acting as the contact point for data subjects and the supervisory authority. The role is defined in Articles 37 to 39 of the GDPR. A DPO must be able to operate independently, report to the highest level of management, and have sufficient resources and expertise.

## What it means for TRC
TRC has a named DPO (or an externally contracted equivalent) who owns the [[GDPR Article 30 - ROPA]], signs off every [[DPIA]], reviews each [[Data Processing Agreement]], and is the escalation point for any [[Personal Data Breach]]. The audit will check the appointment is documented, the DPO has direct access to leadership, and the workload is realistic.

## When it applies
A DPO is mandatory under GDPR Article 37 when processing involves regular and systematic monitoring of data subjects at scale, or large-scale processing of [[Special Category Data]]. Even where not strictly mandatory, TRC has chosen to appoint one because the recruitment activity is data-heavy.
