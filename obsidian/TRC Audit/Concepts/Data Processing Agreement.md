# Data Processing Agreement

## What it is (plain English)
A Data Processing Agreement (DPA) is the written contract between a [[Data Controller]] and a [[Data Processor]] that sets out how the processor will handle personal data on behalf of the controller. It must contain the mandatory clauses listed in [[GDPR Article 28 - Processors]] — purpose, duration, security, confidentiality, sub-processors, audits, and what happens to the data at the end of the engagement. It is usually a standalone document or an addendum to the main services agreement.

## What it means for TRC
TRC must have a DPA on file with every vendor that processes personal data on TRC's behalf, including Anthropic, Microsoft, Railway, Firefish, and Odoo. Each DPA should be reviewed before signature, kept in a central register, and re-reviewed when the supplier publishes a new version. The audit will sample the register and expect to see DPAs that match the supplier list in the [[GDPR Article 30 - ROPA]].

## When it applies
In force since 25 May 2018. Required before any personal data is shared with a processor — not after the fact.
