# GDPR Article 30 - ROPA

## What it is (plain English)
Article 30 requires controllers and processors to keep a written Record of Processing Activities (ROPA). For each processing activity it lists: the purpose, the categories of data subjects and personal data, the recipients (including any outside the EU), retention periods, and a general description of security measures. The under-250-employee exemption is narrow and does not apply when processing is regular, when it includes special categories, or when it can risk people's rights — which is true for almost any recruitment business.

## What it means for TRC
TRC must maintain a current ROPA covering recruitment workflows, HR data, marketing, finance, and the AI-related activities that touch personal data. The audit will ask for the ROPA in its current state and check that each entry maps to a [[Lawful Basis]], to the relevant [[Data Processor]] vendors (Firefish, Odoo, Microsoft, Anthropic, Railway), and to any international transfer documented under [[GDPR Chapter V - International Transfers]].

## When it applies
In force since 25 May 2018. In practice mandatory for TRC and must be kept current at all times.
