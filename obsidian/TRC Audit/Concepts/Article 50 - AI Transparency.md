# Article 50 - AI Transparency

## What it is (plain English)
Article 50 of the EU AI Act sets transparency duties that apply regardless of risk tier. People interacting with an AI system (for example a chatbot) must be told they are dealing with AI unless it is obvious. AI-generated or AI-manipulated text, images, audio, and video — including deepfakes — must be clearly labelled as such. Providers must also make synthetic content machine-detectable where feasible.

## What it means for TRC
Any TRC product or internal tool that talks to a human (a candidate chatbot, an auto-reply, an AI-drafted email sent on behalf of a person) must disclose that AI is involved. AI-generated marketing assets and client deliverables need a visible label or footer. The audit will look for a transparency policy, sample disclosures, and a content-labelling convention applied across [[Claude Code]] outputs, Microsoft Copilot output, and any externally-facing channel.

## When it applies
Applies from 2 August 2026. TRC should roll out the disclosure pattern across all customer-facing surfaces before that date.
