# Article 4 - AI Literacy

## What it is (plain English)
Article 4 of the EU AI Act requires every provider and deployer of an AI system to make sure the staff and contractors who operate or use that system have a sufficient level of "AI literacy." That means they understand, in proportion to their role, what the system does, where it can go wrong, and how to use it responsibly. It is a continuous obligation, not a one-off training tick-box.

## What it means for TRC
TRC must keep a documented record of who at the firm uses AI tools (for example [[Claude Code]] via Anthropic, Microsoft Copilot, or any model deployed on Railway) and what training or briefing they have received. The audit will look for an AI literacy policy, a training log, and evidence that new joiners are inducted before they touch AI workflows. The [[DPO]] or a delegated owner is accountable.

## When it applies
Already in force since 2 February 2025. TRC must be able to demonstrate compliance today.
