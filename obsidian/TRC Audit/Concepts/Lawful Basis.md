# Lawful Basis

## What it is (plain English)
A lawful basis is the legal ground in Article 6 of the GDPR that allows an organisation to process personal data in the first place. The six options are consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, and legitimate interests; one of them must be identified, documented, and communicated to the data subject before processing starts.

## What it means for TRC
Each processing activity in the [[GDPR Article 30 - ROPA]] must name its lawful basis — for example, "contract" for paying a placed candidate, "legitimate interests" for sourcing passive candidates via Firefish, "consent" for marketing emails sent through Odoo. The audit will check that the basis is recorded, that the privacy notice given to candidates and clients matches it, and that legitimate-interests assessments (LIAs) exist where that basis is used.

## When it applies
Already in force. The basis must be fixed before processing begins and cannot be swapped retroactively, so any new TRC workflow (new AI tool, new marketing campaign) requires the [[DPO]] to confirm the basis up front.
