# Transfer Impact Assessment — <recipient>

Structured per EDPB Recommendations 01/2020 on supplementary measures, updated for the EU-US Data Privacy Framework.

## Step 1 — Know your transfer
- What data is transferred, to whom, where, for what purpose?
- Onward transfers (sub-processors)?

## Step 2 — Identify the transfer tool
- [[Standard Contractual Clauses|SCCs]] / DPF / adequacy decision / Article 49 derogation?
- Date of signature and version.

## Step 3 — Assess the third country's law
- Does the recipient country's law materially affect the SCC protections in this specific transfer? Cite primary surveillance laws (e.g. FISA 702, EO 12333 for the US).
- Has the recipient certified to the EU-US DPF? If yes, for which categories of data?

## Step 4 — Supplementary measures
- Encryption in transit and at rest?
- Pseudonymisation?
- Contractual commitments to challenge access requests?
- Operational measures (data minimisation, retention limits)?

## Step 5 — Procedural steps
- Notify the recipient and re-paper if conditions change.
- Suspend transfers if supplementary measures are insufficient.

## Step 6 — Re-evaluation
Review every 12 months and on every material legal development (e.g. CJEU rulings, recipient policy changes, DPF status changes).
