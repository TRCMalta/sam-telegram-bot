# DPO Appointment Assessment

[[GDPR Article 30 - ROPA|GDPR]] Article 37(1) requires a [[DPO]] when **any one** of the following applies. Tick the boxes that apply to TRC.

- [ ] **(a)** Processing is carried out by a **public authority or body**.
- [ ] **(b)** Core activities require **regular and systematic monitoring** of data subjects **on a large scale**.
- [ ] **(c)** Core activities consist of processing **[[Special Category Data]]** (Art. 9) or criminal-offence data **on a large scale**.

## TRC's position

| Test | Applies? | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| (a) public body | No | TRC is a private SME. |
| (b) large-scale monitoring | **Maybe — assess** | If Sam reads Beverly's mailbox continuously plus the Firefish candidate pipeline plus Odoo HR, this can be argued as "regular and systematic monitoring" of TRC workers and candidates. "Large scale" is contextual — EDPB Guidelines WP243 list relevant factors. |
| (c) special category at scale | **Maybe — assess** | If candidate CVs and HR records hold health, trade-union, religious or biometric data and are read in volume by Sam, Art. 37(1)(c) can trigger. |

## Recommended action

1. Governor reads EDPB Guidelines on Data Protection Officers (WP243).
2. Governor records a written assessment on this page including the volumes (workers, candidates, monthly Sam interactions reading personal data).
3. If borderline, **appoint a DPO voluntarily**. Voluntary appointment is treated by IDPC as a positive accountability signal. Notify the IDPC of the appointment if mandatory.

## If appointed

- Name and contact published in the Privacy Notice.
- Independence from operational AI ownership documented.
- Resourced sufficiently to perform the role.
